INTRODUCTION

The Ashanti Kingdom, also known as the Ashanti Empire, originated in the 17th century in present-day Ghana. It was founded by Osei Tutu together with the great Okomfo Anokye who called the upon spiritual forces to unite the Akan states under Osei Tutu's leadership. The Golden stool created by Okomfo Anokye is believed to embody the soul of Ashantis and symbolizes unity.

SYMBOLS

GOLDEN STOOL

The Golden stool also known as "Sika Dwa Kofi" name implies is made of gold. It represents the unity and sanctity of the Ashanti people. The stool is sacred and it's seldom seen.


ASANTEMAN FLAG

The flag consists of three colors;

  • The first color that's yellow represents our richnes(gold)
  • Followed by black which represents our skin color and nature

    • In the middle of the black there's the "denkyemky3" which symbolizes the power of the Golden stool,asanteman and Otumfuo(king)

  • The green color depicts our forest.

In all the flag means "sikani bibinii a ote ne kwayemu "meaning a wealthy black man living in his forest.


ADINKAR SYMBOL

Adinkra symbols are traditional symbols used by Ashantis. These symbols have unique meanings and values and also sometimes used as proverbs .There are about 100 Adinkra symbols and they mostly seen in fabrics.

CULTURE

FOOD

Ashanti food includes a variety of dishes reflecting the rich culinary heritage of the Ashanti people. Some Ashanti foods are:

  • Fufu(pounded cassava and plantain)
  • Ogoo (mashed cocoyam)
  • Akyekye(couscous)
  • Aprapransa(a dish made of corn flour and palm soup often eaten with crab)

    • And many more

    Our main food stuffs grown are cassava,plantain, yam,maize,tomato, pepper, maize.

FESTIVALS


AKWASIDAE

It is celebrated every six weeks and involves homage to the asantehene and ancestors. Thos festival unite Ashantis as they come together . It involves vibrant cultural expressions including traditional music dance and drumming.


AWUKUDAE

Awuku means Wednesday so awukudae is celebrated when akwasidae falls on Wednesday.

Akwasidae and awukudae is the main types of the adae festival. The dates vary and it follows a lunar calendar.


ADAEKESE

"Kese"means grand so adaekese is the grand form of awukudae and akwasidae. It is celebrated twice every year in the Ashanti calendar.

MUSICAL INSTRUMENTS

The Ashanti people, who are part of the Akan ethnic group in Ghana, have a rich musical heritage with traditional instruments. Some examples of Ashanti traditional instruments include:


1.Atumpan:

A pair of male, female talking drum. Played by the master drummer in Adowa and as part of the ensemble in Fontonfrom. Played in pairs Male drum to your left and female to your right on a frame that tilts the drums away from you.


2.Fontomfrom:

The name of the largest of the Ashanti drums, often five foot high and 2 foot wide. Played in pairs by two.


3.Torowa:

Beaded gourd. Made from hollowed out gourd beads on the outside tied in a net. Produces sound when with on the palms or laps.


4.Seperewa:

An old Akan harp


5.Prepensua:

A box Piano with three to five notes made of bamboo or metal. Notes played with fingers.


6.Petia:

Support drum used in Adowa . High pitched and played with two straight sticks.


7.Firikyiwa:

Castanet


8.Apentema:

Also known as ampae.A fairly small sonorous drum used as a support drum. Played by hands.


9.Donno:

Looking like an hour glass,donno is a drum played under.the armpit with sticks attached with strings from the top to the base.


10.Dawuro:

It is a slit bell which produces sound when struck by a metal rod.

MUSIC AND DANCE


ADOWA

It is one of the most renowned dances performed by Akan which Ashantis fall under.

HISTORY BEHIND ADOWA

History has it that in the early days of warfare,Nana Aberewa Tutuwaa an Ashanti queen mother fell very ill. To heal her one had to get a life antelope from the wild imorder to pacify the gods to heal her.This was revealed by the cheif priest. The group who went to capture this antelope saw the enticing movement of the antelope and mimicked the dance to the entire household how the antelope portrayed such movements. The old women imitated and improvised the movements hence becoming one of the most popular dances.


ASAADUA

Asaadua was once a popular recreation musical type among the Akan people of Ghana. Like other popular entertainment music, which evolves from the ingenuity of some veteran traditional musicians, Asaadua started as a youth recreational music for the men of the Akan tradition. The name Assadua evoving from the Asaa tree, relates to the gay and pleasant nature of the dance. The Asaa is a sweet fruit tree commonly found in forest region of Ghana. Asaadua is a dance for enjoyment and pleasure.


FONTONFROM

AFontomfrom is the name of a drum as well as a dances. Fontonfrom is performed often during enstoolment and other special functions.Fontomfrom is complex. It is a series of warrior dances.


KETE

Kete is commonly found in the royal courts of traditional Akan communities. It is performed in the courts of every chief whose status entitles him to be carried in a palanquin. There are three parts of the performance: Drum music, pipe interludes, and vocal counterpart of the pipe tunes. Adaban also called Topre is used when the chief has to perform the ceremonial “shooting dance”. Apente is used mostly for processions.


NTAN

ANtan is famous amongst the Ashanti communities. It is a haughty dance. It laughs at the Ashatis that come back from the south. Those that had an encounter with the whites and now think they are better than the rest. The dance mimick how they walk with their walking sticks and umbrellas.
